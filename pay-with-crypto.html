Why no card?

Cards introduce friction that doesn't belong in infrastructure. Chargebacks can get accounts suspended with no warning. Issuing banks block "hosting" transactions in certain regions. Card details stored on a server are a liability.

Crypto removes all of that. A transaction is a transaction — it doesn't know what country you're in, what your bank thinks about VPS providers, or whether your card has expired. Your service keeps running as long as your balance covers it.

bithost has been running on crypto payments since 2014. No cards, no KYC, no questions.

Common questions

Which cryptocurrencies can I pay with?

Over 300 coins and tokens, including Bitcoin, Ethereum, Litecoin, XRP, Dogecoin, Solana, Tron, and Monero, plus stablecoins like USDT and USDC. Tokens are supported across major networks including BNB Chain, Polygon, Arbitrum, Optimism, and Base. The full, current list is shown on the deposit screen in your dashboard.

Can I pay for a VPS with Bitcoin?

Yes. bithost accepts Bitcoin both on-chain and via the Lightning Network. On-chain deposits credit after 1 confirmation, typically 10-20 minutes. Lightning deposits are instant.

Is there a minimum top-up amount?

The minimum top-up amount is $20 USD.

How long until my deposit is credited?

Lightning is instant. On-chain BTC credits after 1 network confirmation. ETH, LTC, DOGE, USDT, USDC and most other coins credit after their standard confirmation time — usually under 5 minutes.

Do I need to verify my identity?

No KYC is required. bithost only asks for an email address — no ID, no passport, no personal documents.
